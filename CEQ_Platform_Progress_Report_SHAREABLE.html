Summary

This report is to update the team on where we are with the CEQ AI Coaching Platform. The aim is to give everyone a clear view of what's been built, what's been improved since our March meeting, and where we now sit competitively to take it to the next stage commercially.

I've split it into two parts so it's easier to follow. Part 1 covers the build that came out of our March planning meeting: the full quantitative and qualitative critique engine, branded PDF reports, the dual submission workflows, video integration and the new accordion-based report layout. Part 2 covers the further enhancements I've made through early April: tightening up the critique engine, promoting the Question Generator to a proper full-screen mode, rebuilding the Ask ARIA experience to be much more readable, and (most significantly) loading the full CEQ Executive Coaching Methodology in as a layered knowledge module. That last one is what sets the platform apart from any generic AI coaching tool currently on the market.

Headline: The platform now delivers a full quantitative and qualitative AI critique (OUCH! Habit Counter, Positive Coaching Behaviours, GROW Tracker, Speaking Time Analysis, Methodology Fidelity), a Question Generator that produces ready-to-use GROW questions for any meeting, an Ask ARIA reference mode with paginated answers, and, most importantly, a coaching engine grounded in CEQ's own proprietary methodology rather than generic best practice.

Where the platform now stands

ARIA is no longer just "an AI that asks open questions and tracks GROW". With the methodology now loaded, she now functions as a digital extension of CEQ's coaching IP, able to recognise which mode a client is in, steer them through the Personal Map, and appraise sessions against our own ritual standards. Combined with OUCH!, GROW, the 7 Triggers, SuperListening, Logical Levels and BreakThrough (all of which remain the primary engine), our USP is that the platform cannot be replicated without access to CEQ's proprietary methodology.

1. CEQ AI Critique Engine

OUCH! Habit Counter

Quantitative count of Closed Questions, Statements and Multiple Inputs, each with two or three real examples lifted directly from the transcript and shown in a red-themed table.

Positive Coaching Behaviours

Separate green-themed table tracking Open Questions, Silence Held and Coachee's Language Used (reflective language detection - a core SuperListening skill).

GROW Model Tracker

Tracks each GROW stage (Goal, Reality, Options, Will) with status indicators and evidence quoted from the conversation, so gaps are obvious at a glance.

Strict Counting Rules

6. Live VC Recording - Teams & Zoom Integration

A significant part of the recent work has been extending the platform to handle live coaching conversations held over video calls. Most of our real coaching is now delivered on Teams or Zoom rather than face to face, so it was important the platform could capture those sessions and run them through the same critique engine as anything recorded directly inside the app.

Teams & Zoom Capture

Users can now record their video coaching sessions on either Microsoft Teams or Zoom and upload the resulting file (audio or video) into the platform for ARIA to transcribe and critique.

Step-by-Step Instructions

A clear, plain-English instruction panel walks the user through enabling local recording in Teams or Zoom, locating the saved file afterwards, and uploading it into the platform - so no prior tech knowledge is needed.

Same Critique Engine

Once uploaded, the recording goes through exactly the same Whisper transcription and CEQ AI critique pipeline as in-app recordings. The coach gets the same OUCH! / GROW / Speaking Time / Methodology Fidelity report, regardless of where the session was recorded.

Coverage of Live Client Work

This closes the gap between the platform and how coaching is actually delivered day to day. Coaches can now receive AI feedback on their real client sessions, not only on practice scenarios recorded inside the app.

7. AI Policy Declaration & GDPR Signposting

Because real coaching conversations are now being recorded, transcribed and analysed by AI, it was important to be upfront with users about how their data is handled. A new AI Policy declaration pop-up now appears at the relevant point in the workflow, with clear GDPR information alongside it.

AI Policy Declaration Pop-Up

A clear, plain-English pop-up explains that the platform uses AI (OpenAI Whisper for transcription, Anthropic Claude for critique) to process recordings, and asks the user to acknowledge before proceeding.

GDPR Signposting

The pop-up signposts how recordings, transcripts and reports are stored and handled, who has access, and the user's rights under GDPR. Links out to the full CEQ data policy for anyone who wants the detail.

Coachee Consent Reminder

Coaches are reminded that any third party in the recording (a coachee, a colleague) must have given informed consent before the session is uploaded for AI analysis. Built into the upload flow rather than buried in terms.

One-Time Acknowledgement

The declaration is shown the first time a user uploads or records a session, then remembered against their account so it isn't repeated unnecessarily on subsequent use.

Part 2
April - Further Enhancements
Refinements to the critique engine, the promotion of the Question Generator to a full mode, an Ask ARIA rebuild, and the loading of the CEQ Executive Coaching Methodology as a layered knowledge module.

9. Critique Engine - Accuracy & Fidelity Refinements

With more real recordings going through the engine, I've been able to tune it so the output accurately reflects the coaching conversation. The OUCH! Habit Counter now ignores social pleasantries like greetings and sign-offs, so the Statements and Closed Questions counts only reflect genuine coaching utterances. The GROW Tracker has been refocused so it appraises the coach rather than the coachee, with a "Coach's Inputs" column that quotes the actual questions and prompts used to steer the conversation through Goal, Reality, Options and Will. Speaking Time Analysis is now anchored to the authoritative recording duration, so the totals always add up correctly. The result is a critique the team can trust and defend in front of a coachee. (Technical detail of the underlying fixes is in the Appendix.)

9.1 "Ask CEQ" renamed to "Ask ARIA"

Every reference to "Ask CEQ" across the navigation, dashboard cards, page headers, mode pills and chat welcome screens has been renamed to "Ask ARIA". "CEQ frameworks" is deliberately preserved (e.g. "Our AI assistant ARIA will give feedback using CEQ coaching frameworks") so the terminology stays accurate.

10. Question Generator - Promoted to a Full Mode

The Question Generator previously sat as a small collapsible panel that looked more like a title than a button. It has now been promoted to a fourth mode pill alongside Coaching, Practice Partner and Ask ARIA, with a full-screen panel of its own.

  • Coach / Coachee role toggle. The Coach role generates 10 open questions for the user to ask in a real upcoming meeting, addressed to the other party. The Coachee role generates self-reflection questions for the user to prepare themselves before being coached. Two clearly distinct outputs from the same scenario input.
  • GROW-structured output. Always returns 10 questions split as 2 × Goal, 3 × Reality, 3 × Options, 2 × Will, with a brief italicised coaching tip after each question explaining why it works.
  • Branded results card. Questions render in a thin navy outline card with each question in its own gold-bordered inline box, matching the look of the OUCH! and GROW analysis tables.
  • Download PDF and Email. Generated questions can be exported as a styled PDF with CEQ branding and the original scenario context, or emailed straight from the panel.
  • Layout fix. The chat container's fixed height was previously trapping the scroll inside the panel. Question Generator mode now releases that constraint so the page scrolls naturally with longer outputs.

11. Practice Partner - User-Directed Roleplay

The Practice Partner mode has been opened up so the user is properly in control of the session. Previously the roleplay scenarios were quite prescriptive; the user can now set the terms of the practice session themselves before ARIA steps into role.

  • Coach / Coachee toggle. A simple pill toggle lets the user choose which side of the conversation they want to practise. Pick "Coach" and ARIA plays the coachee, giving the user a safe space to rehearse their questioning, listening and GROW steering. Pick "Coachee" and ARIA plays the coach, so the user can experience being on the receiving end of a CEQ-style session.
  • Free-form scenario direction. The user can type any scenario they like into the scenario box, in their own words: a tricky direct report, a board-level conflict, a personal career crossroads, a difficult feedback conversation, anything at all. ARIA picks up the context and plays the relevant role authentically against it, rather than being limited to a fixed list of pre-canned scenarios.
  • Methodology-aware roleplay. Because the CEQ Executive Coaching Methodology is loaded into Practice Partner mode (see Section 10), ARIA can play a C-level coachee far more authentically when the scenario calls for it - referencing OKRs, sponsor dynamics, vision and mission alignment - rather than defaulting to generic "I'm stressed at work" roleplay.

12. Ask ARIA - Speed, Formatting & Pagination

Ask ARIA used to dump a wall of text and take a noticeable pause before doing it. Three changes have transformed the experience:

  • Markdown rendering. Replies now render with proper headings, paragraphs, bullets, numbered lists, bold and italics, blockquotes and clean spacing. A lightweight client-side parser handles all of this without any external dependencies.
  • Bitesize pagination. Multi-item answers - for example "What are the 7 Triggers?" or "Tell me about OUCH!" - are now paginated, one item per page, with "← Back" and "Next →" buttons and a "Page X of Y" counter. Single-concept questions still come back on a single page.
  • Hardened error surfacing. If anything goes wrong, the user now sees an inline diagnostic in the panel rather than a silent failure, which makes any future debugging immediate.

13. CEQ Executive Coaching Methodology - Loaded as Layered Knowledge

This is, strategically, the most significant enhancement. The full CEQ Executive Coaching Methodology document, covering Sections A to D - the Personal Map, the Two Foundation Stones and Four Buckets of Leadership, Working with the Client, and In the Session - has been loaded into a new getCEQMethodology() function in the backend and selectively injected into the relevant ARIA modes. All pricing detail has been stripped out entirely as commercially sensitive, and never reaches the model.

Why this matters - the CEQ USP

This single change moves the platform from being "another AI coaching app" to being a faithful digital extension of CEQ's proprietary executive coaching IP. ARIA now coaches, critiques and generates questions through the same lens that CEQ's human coaches use with C-suite clients - rather than through generic open-question best practice. No competitor can replicate this without access to the methodology.

13.1 Where it adds real value to ARIA

  1. Coaching mode gets a much richer mental model. ARIA already coaches well at the question level. With the methodology loaded, it can recognise which phase the user is in (Taking Stock vs Operating Model vs In-Session work) and steer accordingly - for example a brand-new user gets gently walked through "Who am I / Where am I now / How did I get here / Where am I going" rather than being thrown straight into goal-setting.
  2. Critique gains a second lens. The critique today judges the coach's technique (open vs closed, OUCH habits, GROW coverage). With the methodology loaded, it can additionally flag methodology fidelity - for example "no contracting at the start", "no Download ritual offered", "session ran past time" (which we now have the duration for), "no permission-to-advise framing". This is what sets the platform apart from every other AI coaching tool on the market.
  3. Question Generator can be methodology-aware. If the user says "first session with a new client", the generator biases toward the Personal Map questions; if they say "my client is stuck and miserable", it can diagnose Endure / Overthrow / Escape and generate accordingly. A meaningful improvement with no new UI required.
  4. Ask ARIA becomes the canonical reference for "what is the Download?", "what are the 4 Buckets?", "Endure vs Overthrow vs Escape" - content it previously didn't have access to and would otherwise have had to guess at.
  5. Practice Partner can simulate a C-level coachee far more authentically - referencing OKRs, sponsor dynamics, LTIPs, Vision/Mission alignment - instead of generic "I'm stressed at work" roleplay.

13.2 What it should NOT do - the disruption risk and how it's mitigated

  • It must not replace OUCH!, GROW or the 7 Triggers as the primary frameworks. Those are the engine. The methodology is an additional layer that sits above them. Mitigated by an explicit guardrail at the bottom of getCEQMethodology() stating that the methodology supplements - never replaces - the core frameworks.
  • Pricing detail is commercially sensitive. All pricing references have been stripped entirely from the methodology block passed to the model. They do not exist in any prompt ARIA can see.
  • The "Download" verbatim script is a coach's tool, not something ARIA should ever recite at a coachee. It's gated behind a clear guardrail: only quoted verbatim when the user is explicitly asking how to run or open a session, with a mandatory prelude - "This is the verbatim CEQ Download opener used by coaches at the start of every session - use it as written, it's designed as a state-shift ritual."
  • Don't bloat every prompt with the full text. Token cost would balloon and the core frameworks would be diluted. Mitigated by selective injection - the methodology is appended only to the modes where it adds value (Coaching, Practice, Ask ARIA, Question Generator) and used as hidden reference inside Critique.

13.3 Implementation - zero core-engine disruption

A new getCEQMethodology() helper in the backend returns the methodology as a structured constant (Sections A/B/C/D, with the pricing block excluded). The system prompt builder then appends a condensed methodology summary to the prompt only for the modes where it adds value:

  • Coaching mode → appends the Personal Map and Working with Client sections (where the Endure / Overthrow / Escape diagnosis is particularly valuable)
  • Critique → appends the "In the Session" rituals as additional appraisal criteria, added to (not replacing) OUCH! / GROW
  • Question Generator → appends Personal Map prompts when the scenario sounds first-session or exploratory
  • Ask ARIA → full methodology available as reference (still no pricing)
  • Practice Partner → appends the C-level context flavour
  • No change to the OUCH! / GROW / 7 Triggers blocks - they stay first and authoritative

The critique gets one new optional section in the report: "Methodology Fidelity" - a green/amber/red tracker against five elements (Contracting, Download ritual, Time-keeping, Permissioned advice, Between-session offer). It is strictly additive - nothing existing changes. Importantly, the section is conditional: it only appears when the recording is at least 8 minutes long and the conversation looks like a real coaching session with enough material to fairly judge at least two of the criteria. Short drills and practice fragments don't trigger it, which protects the report from bloat.

Appendix
Issues Encountered & Resolutions
Reference material - the technical issues hit during the March build and how each one was resolved. Included for completeness; not required reading.
Issue Root Cause Resolution
"Record Live" button not working Stray duplicate code at the top level of the script (outside any function) threw a ReferenceError on pageName, killing all JavaScript below it. Removed the stray duplicate lines, which restored all functionality including selectConvMode().
OUCH! counter inflated by social pleasantries Greetings, "how are you?", sign-offs and weather small-talk were being counted as Statements and Closed Questions, which skewed the totals upward and made the report feel unfair to the coach. Added explicit exclusion rules in the system prompt so pleasantries are filtered out of the quantitative counts. They still get acknowledged in the qualitative OUCH! analysis as good conversation management.
GROW Tracker pointing at the wrong person Evidence column was quoting things the coachee said, when the tracker is supposed to appraise the coach's steering of the conversation. Renamed the column to "Coach's Inputs" and rewrote the prompt so it only quotes the coach's questions and prompts. If the coach didn't actively steer into a stage, it's now correctly marked Partial or Missing.
Speaking Time totals exceeding recording length The AI was estimating Coach/Coachee minutes from word count with no anchor to the real recording duration. A 3-minute clip was reporting 7.2 minutes total. Passed the authoritative durationSeconds into the critique prompt as a hard constraint, with explicit maths rules ("Coach + Coachee + Silence must sum to total"), a worked example and a deliberate "bad example". All three critique call sites updated.
"Could not decode String" error on video upload GAS Utilities.base64Decode() fails when the base64 string contains whitespace, newlines or the data URI prefix. Added a cleaning step that strips the data:...;base64, prefix and all whitespace before decoding.
Video redirect to Dashboard instead of Practice Videos The deep-link handler only checked for ?page=conversations and ignored other page values. Made the deep-link handler generic - it now supports any page value passed via the template variable.
Practice Videos not showing recordings getPracticeVideos() only matched the logged-in user's email, but the standalone recorder saves with empty/anonymous email. Added || rowEmail === 'anonymous' || rowEmail === '' to the filter condition.
Closed questions over-counted (4 instead of 2) The AI was counting self-referential statements like "I realised I asked closed questions" as closed questions themselves. Added much stricter rules in the system prompt with explicit positive and negative examples of what constitutes a closed question.
Table headers ghosted/invisible GAS iframe CSS sanitisation was overriding var(--ceq-primary) CSS custom properties on table headers. Replaced CSS variables with direct hex values (#1a1952) and added !important to ensure visibility.
Download Report, Coach Review & Master Accordion all broken All three functions were defined as regular function declarations inside an IIFE, making them locally scoped and invisible to HTML onclick handlers (which require global scope). Changed all three from function name() { to window.name = function() {, matching the pattern used by all other working functions.
Question Generator results not appearing The .coach-container had a fixed height with overflow:hidden designed for the chat layout, which clipped the Question Generator panel below the visible area. Releases the height and overflow constraints when Question Generator mode is active; restores them when switching back to chat-based modes.
Speaking Time exceeded recording length The AI was estimating Coach/Coachee minutes from word count without any anchor to the actual recording duration, producing impossible totals. Passed the authoritative durationSeconds into the critique prompt with explicit maths rules: Coach + Coachee + Silence must sum to total. Three call sites updated.
Pattern noted: Two of the original issues shared the same root cause - JavaScript scoped incorrectly inside the GAS iframe environment. Worth remembering as a key architectural rule: any function called by an HTML onclick handler must be attached to the window object when inside an IIFE wrapper.
